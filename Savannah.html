Welcome to Savannah

City Overview

Savannah, Georgia, a historic gem nestled along the picturesque banks of the Savannah River, captivates visitors with its timeless charm and Southern hospitality. Established in 1733 by British colonists led by General James Oglethorpe, Savannah stands as one of the oldest cities in the United States. With its cobblestone streets, moss-draped oaks, and meticulously preserved antebellum architecture, Savannah exudes an old-world elegance that enchants all who wander its historic squares. As of the latest estimates, Savannah's population hovers around 147,000, making it one of Georgia's largest cities and a vibrant cultural center renowned for its arts, cuisine, and coastal beauty. Incorporated in 1789, Savannah boasts a rich heritage that beckons visitors to explore its storied past while embracing its dynamic present.

(Source: Wikipedia)

Region and Classification

Savannah, Georgia, situated in the southeastern part of the state, epitomizes the essence of Southern elegance and coastal charm. As the oldest city in Georgia, Savannah stands as a testament to centuries of history and cultural heritage. Classified as a major metropolitan area the state, Savannah boasts a diverse economy driven by industries such as tourism, shipping, manufacturing, and education. Its strategic location along the Savannah River has historically made it a vital port city, fostering trade and commerce with the broader region and beyond. With its well-preserved historic districts, thriving arts scene, and stunning natural landscapes, Savannah continues to enchant visitors and residents alike, earning its reputation as a gem of the Georgia coast.

(Source: New Georgia Encyclopedia/Georgia Geographic Regions)

Average Income and Economy

In Savannah, Georgia, the median household income typically reflects its diverse economic landscape and stands in line with the broader state average. While specific figures may vary over time, Savannah's median income tends to align closely with the statewide median income level. In 2022, the median household income of Savannah households was $54,748. The average income for Georgia is $71,355. As a major city in Georgia, Savannah benefits from a range of industries, including tourism, shipping, manufacturing, and education, which contribute to its economic stability. While the city's median income may fluctuate, it generally mirrors the statewide trends, showcasing Savannah's position as an integral part of Georgia's economic fabric.
