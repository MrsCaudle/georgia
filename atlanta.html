Welcome to Atlanta

City Overview

Atlanta, located in Fulton County, Georgia, is that capital and largest city in Georgia with a diverse population and vibrant culture. As of 2022, the population within the city limits of Atlanta was 498,715. However, the Atlanta city limits is only the core of the greater Atlanta Metroplitan which boasts a whopping 6.3 million people. Making it the 6th larges metroplitan area in the U.S. Established in 1847, the city boasts a rich history of everything from civil war to civil rights that is woven into its unique character and cultural that exists today.

(Source: Wikipedia)

Region and Classification

Atlanta, the capital city of Georgia, serves as the vibrant hub of the state and the broader Southeastern region of the United States. Positioned in the north-central part of Georgia, Atlanta is classified as a major metropolitan area, boasting a diverse population, bustling economy, and cultural significance. As the economic epicenter of the Southeast, Atlanta is a key player in industries ranging from finance and technology to entertainment and transportation. With its world-class amenities, renowned universities, and thriving arts scene, Atlanta has earned its reputation as a dynamic and influential city on both a regional and national scale.

(Source: New Georgia Encyclopedia/Georgia Geographic Regions)

Average Income and Economy

In Atlanta, the median household income stands as a testament to its economic prowess, typically exceeding that of the broader state of Georgia. In 2022, the median household income of Atlanta households was $77,655. The average income for Georgia is $71,355. With a median household income consistently surpassing the statewide average, Atlanta reflects its status as a major economic hub within the state. This higher median income level in Atlanta is often attributed to the city's diverse array of industries, including finance, technology, and healthcare, which provide ample opportunities for employment and career advancement. As a result, Atlanta continues to attract professionals seeking higher earning potential and a vibrant urban lifestyle, further solidifying its position as a leading economic center in the Southeast.

(Source: US Census)
